Data visualizations

Data Conclusions

The original aim for the visualizations was to provide a more easy to understand and easily interpretable look at the overall university review data. Essentially allowing a graphical summary of different parts of the data, such as the overall rating for the University of Lincoln over the previous years. However, it was soon found after basic data analysis that the data was limited in the visualizations able to be derived from it.

Dataset Limitations and Considerations

    There is a heavy amount of reviews in 2019 but very little in 2022 and 2021 This is important to keep in mind during analysis, as when creating visualizations comparing the data from different years, e.g. mean overall rating for the University of lincoln from 2017 - 2022, having more reviews in 2019 will result in a more accurate mean rating. However, for years such as 2022 and 2021, there is a lot less, so the visualizations will be less accurate to the true student experience.



  • When creating visualizations comparing the different courses at Lincoln ("Mean overall rating for courses from the University of Lincoln"), it was found this visualization initially wasn't very effective as many courses have only 1 or few reviews compared to other courses that have many. This can impact the plot as you may have 1 course with 1 review of 5 stars, resulting in a mean of 5, however in actual fact the course might not be great and is being skewed by the view of 1 person. This limitation/issue was combatted by displaying the number of reviews for each course when you hover over the course on the chart.

  • A final limitation when performing analysis on our data was the high amount of non-quantitative data (text based). Although this form of data helps provided greater insights into the university experience, it provides little value in the visualizations. That is why we decided to create 2 word clouds a thumbs up and thumbs down shaped one. The thumbs up word cloud is created using the reviews with a overall rating of 4 or higher, and the thumbs down word cloud is created using the reviews with a overall rating of less then 2.
